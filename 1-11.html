f. GENERALIZE
Write the prime factorization of 10, 100, 1000, and 10,000. What patterns do you see in the prime factorizations of these numbers?

Solution:

The prime factorizations are: $$10 = 2^1 \cdot 5^1$$ $$100 = 10 \cdot 10 = (2 \cdot 5) \cdot (2 \cdot 5) = 2^2 \cdot 5^2$$ $$1000 = 10 \cdot 10 \cdot 10 = 2^3 \cdot 5^3$$ $$10,000 = 10^4 = 2^4 \cdot 5^4$$

Pattern: The exponent of the prime factors (2 and 5) is exactly the same as the number of zeros in the standard number.

2.
The African white rhinoceros can reach a height of about \(5\frac{1}{2}\) feet. How many inches is \(5\frac{1}{2}\) feet?

Answer: 66 inches

We know that \(1 \text{ foot} = 12 \text{ inches}\).
First, convert the whole feet: $$5 \times 12 = 60 \text{ inches}$$ Next, convert the half foot: $$\frac{1}{2} \times 12 = 6 \text{ inches}$$ Total: $$60 + 6 = 66 \text{ inches}$$
3.
Jenny shot 10 free throws and made 6. What fraction of her shots did she make? What percent of her shots did she make?

Fraction: \(\frac{3}{5}\)

Percent: \(60\%\)

Fraction: She made 6 out of 10. $$\frac{6}{10} = \frac{3}{5} \text{ (reduced)}$$ Percent: $$\frac{6}{10} = \frac{60}{100} = 60\%$$
4.
Make a factor tree for 40. Then write the prime factorization of 40.

Prime Factorization: \(2^3 \cdot 5\)

Example Factor Tree:
  • 40 can be split into \(4 \times 10\)
  • 4 splits into \(2 \times 2\) (both prime)
  • 10 splits into \(2 \times 5\) (both prime)
Collecting all the prime tips: \(2, 2, 2, 5\)
Result: \(2 \cdot 2 \cdot 2 \cdot 5 = 2^3 \cdot 5\)

6.
Write \(2\frac{2}{3}\) as an improper fraction. Then multiply the improper fraction by \(\frac{3}{8}\). What is the product?

Improper Fraction: \(\frac{8}{3}\)

Product: \(1\)

Convert to improper fraction: $$2\frac{2}{3} = \frac{(2 \times 3) + 2}{3} = \frac{8}{3}$$ Multiply: $$\frac{8}{3} \times \frac{3}{8} = \frac{24}{24} = 1$$
7.
Four of the ten marbles in the bag are red. If one marble is drawn from the bag, what ratio expresses the probability that the marble will not be red?

Answer: \(\frac{3}{5}\) (or 6 out of 10)

Total marbles = 10.
Red marbles = 4.
Not red = \(10 - 4 = 6\).
Probability (Not Red) = \(\frac{6}{10}\).
Reduced fraction: \(\frac{3}{5}\).
8.
Find the unknown number: \(8\frac{1}{2} + 1\frac{1}{3} + 2\frac{1}{6}\)

Answer: \(12\)

Find a common denominator (LCD is 6): $$8\frac{1}{2} = 8\frac{3}{6}$$ $$1\frac{1}{3} = 1\frac{2}{6}$$ $$2\frac{1}{6} = 2\frac{1}{6}$$ Add the fractions: \(\frac{3}{6} + \frac{2}{6} + \frac{1}{6} = \frac{6}{6} = 1\)
Add the whole numbers: \(8 + 1 + 2 = 11\)
Total: \(11 + 1 = 12\)
9.
Find the unknown number: \(\frac{1}{12} + \frac{1}{6} + \frac{1}{2}\)

Answer: \(\frac{3}{4}\)

Find a common denominator (LCD is 12): $$\frac{1}{12} = \frac{1}{12}$$ $$\frac{1}{6} = \frac{2}{12}$$ $$\frac{1}{2} = \frac{6}{12}$$ Add numerators: $$\frac{1 + 2 + 6}{12} = \frac{9}{12}$$ Reduce the fraction (divide by 3): $$\frac{3}{4}$$

14.
Compare: \(\frac{1}{2} - \frac{1}{3} \bigcirc \frac{2}{3} - \frac{1}{2}\)

Answer: \(=\) (Equal)

Solve the left side: $$\frac{1}{2} - \frac{1}{3} = \frac{3}{6} - \frac{2}{6} = \frac{1}{6}$$ Solve the right side: $$\frac{2}{3} - \frac{1}{2} = \frac{4}{6} - \frac{3}{6} = \frac{1}{6}$$ Both sides equal \(\frac{1}{6}\), so they are equal.

16.
What is the total cost of two dozen erasers that are priced at 50¢ each if 8% sales tax is added? Describe a way to perform the calculation mentally.

Answer: $12.96

Calculation:
2 dozen = 24 erasers.
Cost = \(24 \times \$0.50 = \$12.00\).
Tax = \(8\% \text{ of } \$12.00 = 0.08 \times 12 = \$0.96\).
Total = \(\$12.00 + \$0.96 = \$12.96\).

Mental Math Strategy:
Think of 50¢ as half a dollar. 24 erasers cost 12 dollars. To add 8% tax, calculate 8 cents for every dollar. \(12 \times 8 \text{ cents} = 96 \text{ cents}\). Add that to the $12 to get $12.96.

26.
Write the prime factorization of 375 and of 1000. What method did you use?

375: \(3 \cdot 5^3\) (or \(3 \cdot 5 \cdot 5 \cdot 5\))

1000: \(2^3 \cdot 5^3\) (or \(2 \cdot 2 \cdot 2 \cdot 5 \cdot 5 \cdot 5\))

Method: You can use a factor tree or division by primes.
For 375: Ends in 5, divide by 5 \(\rightarrow 75\). \(75 \div 5 = 15\). \(15 \div 5 = 3\). Factors: \(5, 5, 5, 3\).
For 1000: \(10^3 = (2 \cdot 5)^3 = 2^3 \cdot 5^3\).

28.
The radius of the carousel is 15 feet. If the carousel turns around once, a person riding on the outer edge will travel how far? Round the answer to the nearest foot. (Use 3.14 for \(\pi\).)

Answer: 94 feet

You need the Circumference ($C = 2\pi r$ or $C = \pi d$).
Diameter = \(2 \times 15 = 30\) feet.
Calculation: \(3.14 \times 30\).
\(3.14 \times 10 = 31.4\)
\(31.4 \times 3 = 94.2\) feet.
Rounding to the nearest foot: 94 feet.

30.
VERIFY: The prefix "rect-" in rectangle means "right." A rectangle is a "right-angle" shape. Why is every square also a rectangle?

Answer:

A rectangle is defined as a quadrilateral with four right angles. Since a square is defined as a quadrilateral with four equal sides and four right angles, it fits the definition of a rectangle perfectly.
