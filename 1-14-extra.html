Topic #1: Cost & Mixed Fractions Word Problems

Concept: Adding mixed measurements and multiplying by unit cost.

1. Liam buys \(1 \frac{1}{2}\) yards of red fabric, \(2\) yards of blue fabric, and \(\frac{1}{4}\) yard of white fabric. The fabric costs $4.00 per yard. What is the total cost?
Step 1: Add the lengths.
$$ 1\frac{1}{2} + 2 + \frac{1}{4} $$ $$ 1.5 + 2 + 0.25 = 3.75 \text{ yards} $$ Step 2: Multiply by the cost ($4).
$$ 3.75 \times 4 = 15.00 $$ Answer: $15.00
2. A carpenter uses \(3\) feet of oak, \(1 \frac{1}{2}\) feet of pine, and \(\frac{1}{2}\) foot of maple. The wood costs $3.00 per foot. What is the total cost?
Step 1: Add the lengths.
$$ 3 + 1\frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{2} = 3 + 1.5 + 0.5 = 5 \text{ feet} $$ Step 2: Multiply by the cost ($3).
$$ 5 \times 3 = 15 $$ Answer: $15.00
3. Sarah buys \(2 \frac{1}{4}\) lbs of apples, \(1 \frac{3}{4}\) lbs of oranges, and \(1\) lb of pears. Fruit costs $2.00 per lb. How much money does she need?
Step 1: Add the weights.
$$ 2\frac{1}{4} + 1\frac{3}{4} + 1 $$ Combine fractions: \(\frac{1}{4} + \frac{3}{4} = 1\).
Integers: \(2 + 1 + 1 = 4\).
Total: \(4 + 1 = 5 \text{ lbs}\).
Step 2: Multiply by cost ($2).
$$ 5 \times 2 = 10 $$ Answer: $10.00
4. A baker needs \( \frac{1}{2} \) cup sugar, \( 1 \frac{1}{2} \) cups flour, and \( 2 \) cups oats. These ingredients cost $0.50 per cup on average. What is the total cost?
Step 1: Add the amounts.
$$ \frac{1}{2} + 1\frac{1}{2} + 2 = 0.5 + 1.5 + 2 = 4 \text{ cups} $$ Step 2: Multiply by cost ($0.50).
$$ 4 \times 0.50 = 2.00 $$ Answer: $2.00
5. You need \(1\) meter of red wire, \(\frac{3}{4}\) meter of black wire, and \(\frac{1}{4}\) meter of green wire. Wire costs $1.50 per meter. Total cost?
Step 1: Add lengths.
$$ 1 + \frac{3}{4} + \frac{1}{4} = 1 + 1 = 2 \text{ meters} $$ Step 2: Multiply by cost ($1.50).
$$ 2 \times 1.50 = 3.00 $$ Answer: $3.00

Topic #10: Order of Operations with Decimals

Concept: Perform multiplication inside parentheses before subtracting. Formula: \((a \times b) - (c \times d)\)

1. Simplify: \((4 \times 0.5) - (2 \times 0.5)\)
Step 1: Multiply the groups.
\(4 \times 0.5 = 2.0\)
\(2 \times 0.5 = 1.0\)
Step 2: Subtract.
\(2.0 - 1.0 = 1.0\) Answer: 1
2. Simplify: \((3 \times 1.2) - (0.5 \times 4)\)
Step 1: Multiply the groups.
\(3 \times 1.2 = 3.6\)
\(0.5 \times 4 = 2.0\)
Step 2: Subtract.
\(3.6 - 2.0 = 1.6\) Answer: 1.6
3. Simplify: \((0.8 \times 10) - (0.2 \times 5)\)
Step 1: Multiply.
\(0.8 \times 10 = 8\)
\(0.2 \times 5 = 1\)
Step 2: Subtract.
\(8 - 1 = 7\) Answer: 7
4. Simplify: \((1.5 \times 2) - (0.3 \times 3)\)
Step 1: Multiply.
\(1.5 \times 2 = 3.0\)
\(0.3 \times 3 = 0.9\)
Step 2: Subtract.
\(3.0 - 0.9 = 2.1\) Answer: 2.1
5. Simplify: \((5 \times 0.4) - (2 \times 0.4)\)
Step 1: Multiply.
\(5 \times 0.4 = 2.0\)
\(2 \times 0.4 = 0.8\)
Step 2: Subtract.
\(2.0 - 0.8 = 1.2\) Answer: 1.2

Topic #11: Subtracting Mixed Numbers with Borrowing

Concept: When the first fraction is smaller than the second, borrow 1 whole from the integer.

1. Analyze: \(5 \frac{1}{4} - 2 \frac{3}{4}\)
Step 1: Borrow from 5.
\(5\frac{1}{4}\) becomes \(4 + \frac{4}{4} + \frac{1}{4} = 4\frac{5}{4}\).
Step 2: Subtract.
\(4\frac{5}{4} - 2\frac{3}{4} = 2\frac{2}{4}\)
Step 3: Reduce.
\(2\frac{1}{2}\) Answer: \(2\frac{1}{2}\)
2. Analyze: \(4 \frac{1}{3} - 1 \frac{2}{3}\)
Step 1: Borrow from 4.
\(4\frac{1}{3}\) becomes \(3 + \frac{3}{3} + \frac{1}{3} = 3\frac{4}{3}\).
Step 2: Subtract.
\(3\frac{4}{3} - 1\frac{2}{3} = 2\frac{2}{3}\) Answer: \(2\frac{2}{3}\)
3. Analyze: \(6 \frac{1}{8} - 3 \frac{5}{8}\)
Step 1: Borrow from 6.
\(6\frac{1}{8} \rightarrow 5\frac{9}{8}\).
Step 2: Subtract.
\(5\frac{9}{8} - 3\frac{5}{8} = 2\frac{4}{8}\)
Step 3: Reduce.
\(2\frac{1}{2}\) Answer: \(2\frac{1}{2}\)
4. Analyze: \(3 \frac{1}{5} - 1 \frac{3}{5}\)
Step 1: Borrow from 3.
\(3\frac{1}{5} \rightarrow 2\frac{6}{5}\).
Step 2: Subtract.
\(2\frac{6}{5} - 1\frac{3}{5} = 1\frac{3}{5}\) Answer: \(1\frac{3}{5}\)
5. Analyze: \(5 \frac{1}{4} - 2 \frac{7}{8}\)
Step 1: Common Denominator (8).
\(5\frac{1}{4} = 5\frac{2}{8}\).
Problem is now \(5\frac{2}{8} - 2\frac{7}{8}\).
Step 2: Borrow.
\(5\frac{2}{8} \rightarrow 4\frac{10}{8}\).
Step 3: Subtract.
\(4\frac{10}{8} - 2\frac{7}{8} = 2\frac{3}{8}\) Answer: \(2\frac{3}{8}\)

Topic #12: Multiplying Mixed Numbers & Roots

Concept: Convert mixed numbers to improper fractions and solve the square root first.

1. Solve: \(1 \frac{1}{2} \times \sqrt{16}\)
Step 1: Simplify Root.
\(\sqrt{16} = 4\)
Step 2: Convert mixed number.
\(1\frac{1}{2} = \frac{3}{2}\)
Step 3: Multiply.
\(\frac{3}{2} \times 4 = \frac{12}{2} = 6\) Answer: 6
2. Solve: \(2 \frac{1}{4} \times \sqrt{64}\)
Step 1: Simplify Root.
\(\sqrt{64} = 8\)
Step 2: Convert mixed number.
\(2\frac{1}{4} = \frac{9}{4}\)
Step 3: Multiply.
\(\frac{9}{4} \times 8 = \frac{72}{4} = 18\) Answer: 18
3. Solve: \(3 \frac{1}{3} \times \sqrt{36}\)
Step 1: Simplify Root.
\(\sqrt{36} = 6\)
Step 2: Convert mixed number.
\(3\frac{1}{3} = \frac{10}{3}\)
Step 3: Multiply.
\(\frac{10}{3} \times 6 = \frac{60}{3} = 20\) Answer: 20
4. Solve: \(1 \frac{3}{5} \times \sqrt{25}\)
Step 1: Simplify Root.
\(\sqrt{25} = 5\)
Step 2: Convert mixed number.
\(1\frac{3}{5} = \frac{8}{5}\)
Step 3: Multiply.
\(\frac{8}{5} \times 5 = 8\) Answer: 8
5. Solve: \(4 \frac{1}{2} \times \sqrt{4}\)
Step 1: Simplify Root.
\(\sqrt{4} = 2\)
Step 2: Convert mixed number.
\(4\frac{1}{2} = \frac{9}{2}\)
Step 3: Multiply.
\(\frac{9}{2} \times 2 = 9\) Answer: 9
