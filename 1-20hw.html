Lesson Practice

a. In this figure the length of \( \overline{AC} \) is 60 mm and the length of \( \overline{BC} \) is 26 mm. Find the length of \( \overline{AB} \).

Strategy: Use segment subtraction. The points are collinear in the order A-B-C.

We know the total length \( AC = 60 \) mm.
We know the segment \( BC = 26 \) mm.
To find \( AB \), we subtract \( BC \) from \( AC \): \[ AB = AC - BC \] \[ AB = 60 - 26 \]
AB = 34 mm
b. The complement of a \( 60^\circ \) angle is an angle that measures how many degrees?
Complementary angles sum to \( 90^\circ \).
Calculation: \( 90^\circ - 60^\circ = 30^\circ \).
30°
c. The supplement of a \( 60^\circ \) angle is an angle that measures how many degrees?
Supplementary angles sum to \( 180^\circ \).
Calculation: \( 180^\circ - 60^\circ = 120^\circ \).
120°
d. CONCLUDE If two angles are supplementary, can they both be acute? Why or why not?
An acute angle measures less than \( 90^\circ \).
If you add two numbers that are both less than 90, their sum must be less than 180 (e.g., \( 89 + 89 = 178 \)).
Supplementary angles must add up to exactly \( 180^\circ \).
No. The sum of two acute angles is always less than 180°.

Supplementary angles form a straight line (a linear pair).

Angle 1 and Angle 2

Complementary angles add up to \( 90^\circ \).
The diagram shows a right triangle (indicated by the square symbol). The sum of angles in a triangle is \( 180^\circ \). Since the right angle is \( 90^\circ \), the other two angles (2 and 3) must sum to \( 90^\circ \).

Written Practice

1. MODEL Draw a pair of parallel lines. Then draw a second pair of parallel lines that are perpendicular to the first pair. Trace the quadrilateral that is formed... What kind of quadrilateral did you trace?
Two pairs of parallel lines that are perpendicular to each other form four right angles.
A quadrilateral with two pairs of parallel sides and four right angles is a rectangle.
Rectangle
2. CONNECT What is the quotient if the dividend is \( \frac{1}{2} \) and the divisor is \( \frac{1}{8} \)?
The problem is asking for \( \frac{1}{2} \div \frac{1}{8} \).
To divide by a fraction, multiply by its reciprocal: \[ \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{8}{1} \]
\( \frac{1 \times 8}{2 \times 1} = \frac{8}{2} = 4 \)
4
3. Highest temp was \( 136^\circ \)F. Lowest was \( -129^\circ \)F. How many degrees difference?
Difference = High - Low
\( 136 - (-129) \)
Subtracting a negative is the same as adding: \( 136 + 129 \)
265°F
4. ESTIMATE A dollar bill is about 6 inches long. Placed end to end, about how many feet would 1000 dollar bills reach?
Total length in inches = \( 1000 \text{ bills} \times 6 \text{ inches/bill} = 6000 \text{ inches} \).
Convert inches to feet (divide by 12): \[ 6000 \div 12 \]
\( 60 \div 12 = 5 \), so \( 6000 \div 12 = 500 \).
500 feet
5. Write the prime factorization of both the numerator and the denominator of this fraction. Then reduce the fraction. \( \frac{45}{72} \)
Numerator (45): \( 9 \times 5 = 3 \times 3 \times 5 = 3^2 \times 5 \)
Denominator (72): \( 8 \times 9 = (2 \times 2 \times 2) \times (3 \times 3) = 2^3 \times 3^2 \)
Reduce: \[ \frac{3 \times 3 \times 5}{2 \times 2 \times 2 \times 3 \times 3} \] Cancel out the \( 3 \times 3 \) from top and bottom.
Remaining: \( \frac{5}{2 \times 2 \times 2} = \frac{5}{8} \)
Prime Factorization: \( \frac{3^2 \cdot 5}{2^3 \cdot 3^2} \)
Reduced Fraction: \( \frac{5}{8} \)
6. CONCLUDE In quadrilateral QRST, which segment appears to be parallel to \( \overline{RS} \)?
The figure QRST appears to be a parallelogram.
In a parallelogram, opposite sides are parallel.
The side opposite to \( RS \) is \( QT \).
Segment QT

Average = Total Amount ÷ Number of Days
\( \$27.50 \div 10 \)

8. Evaluate: \( \frac{1 \times 2 \times 3 \times 4 \times 5}{1 + 2 + 3 + 4 + 5} \)
Numerator: \( 1 \times 2 = 2 \); \( 2 \times 3 = 6 \); \( 6 \times 4 = 24 \); \( 24 \times 5 = 120 \).
Denominator: \( 1 + 2 = 3 \); \( 3 + 3 = 6 \); \( 6 + 4 = 10 \); \( 10 + 5 = 15 \).
Division: \( 120 \div 15 \).
Calculation: \( 15 \times 8 = 120 \).
8
9. Evaluate: \( 3\frac{1}{2} + 2\frac{3}{4} + 1\frac{5}{8} \)
Find a common denominator, which is 8.
\( 3\frac{1}{2} = 3\frac{4}{8} \)
\( 2\frac{3}{4} = 2\frac{6}{8} \)
\( 1\frac{5}{8} = 1\frac{5}{8} \)
Add whole numbers: \( 3 + 2 + 1 = 6 \).
Add fractions: \( \frac{4+6+5}{8} = \frac{15}{8} \).
Convert improper fraction: \( \frac{15}{8} = 1\frac{7}{8} \).
Combine: \( 6 + 1\frac{7}{8} \).
\( 7\frac{7}{8} \)
10. Find unknown number: \( m + 1\frac{3}{4} = 5\frac{3}{8} \)
Subtract \( 1\frac{3}{4} \) from both sides: \( m = 5\frac{3}{8} - 1\frac{3}{4} \).
Convert to common denominator (8): \( 1\frac{3}{4} = 1\frac{6}{8} \).
Equation: \( 5\frac{3}{8} - 1\frac{6}{8} \).
Borrow from the 5: \( 5\frac{3}{8} = 4\frac{11}{8} \).
Subtract: \( 4\frac{11}{8} - 1\frac{6}{8} = 3\frac{5}{8} \).
\( m = 3\frac{5}{8} \)
11. Find unknown number: \( \frac{3}{4} - f = \frac{1}{3} \)
Rearrange: \( f = \frac{3}{4} - \frac{1}{3} \).
Common denominator is 12.
\( \frac{3}{4} = \frac{9}{12} \) and \( \frac{1}{3} = \frac{4}{12} \).
\( \frac{9}{12} - \frac{4}{12} = \frac{5}{12} \).
\( f = \frac{5}{12} \)
12. Find unknown number: \( \frac{2}{5}w = 1 \)
To isolate \( w \), divide by \( \frac{2}{5} \), which means multiplying by the reciprocal \( \frac{5}{2} \).
\( w = 1 \times \frac{5}{2} = \frac{5}{2} \).
Convert to mixed number: \( 2\frac{1}{2} \).
\( w = 2\frac{1}{2} \) (or 2.5)
13. Find unknown number: \( \frac{8}{25} = \frac{n}{100} \)
Notice that \( 25 \times 4 = 100 \).
Multiply the numerator by the same factor: \( 8 \times 4 = 32 \).
n = 32

15. \( 2\frac{2}{3} \times 1\frac{1}{5} \)

Simplify before multiplying: 6 divided by 3 is 2.
\( \frac{8 \times 2}{1 \times 5} = \frac{16}{5} \).
Convert to mixed number: \( 3\frac{1}{5} \).
\( 3\frac{1}{5} \)

18. EXPLAIN How can you determine whether a counting number is a composite number?
A composite number is a counting number greater than 1 that is not prime.
To determine if it is composite, check if it has any factors other than 1 and itself.
If a number greater than 1 has more than two factors (meaning it can be divided evenly by a number other than 1 and itself), it is a composite number.

Real-World & Mindset

Real-World Connection: Taylor's mother bought two rolls of ribbon. Each roll is 25 yards. Each ribbon is cut to \( 7\frac{1}{2} \) inches. How many ribbons can they make? (Hint: 1 yard = 36 inches).
1. Find total length in yards: 2 rolls × 25 yards = 50 yards.
2. Convert to inches: \( 50 \text{ yards} \times 36 \text{ inches/yard} = 1800 \text{ inches} \).
3. Divide by ribbon length: Ribbon length = \( 7.5 \) inches (or \( \frac{15}{2} \)). \[ 1800 \div 7.5 \] Easier with fractions: \[ 1800 \div \frac{15}{2} = 1800 \times \frac{2}{15} \]
Calculation: \( 1800 \div 15 = 120 \). \( 120 \times 2 = 240 \).
240 ribbons
Motivating Mindset: Terry does not know how to solve a problem. What should Terry do?
A. Skip the problem.
B. Get the answer from another student.
C. Ask for help from someone who understands.
Option A (Skip) leads to gaps in learning.
Option B (Copy) does not help Terry learn the method.
Option C (Ask for help) allows Terry to understand the concept and solve future problems.
C. Ask for help from someone who understands.
