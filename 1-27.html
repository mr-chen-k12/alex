Problem 1
What is the average of 4.2, 2.61, and 3.6?
Answer: 3.47

To find the average, sum the numbers and divide by the count of numbers.

1. Sum: \( 4.2 + 2.61 + 3.6 = 10.41 \)

2. Count: There are 3 numbers.

3. Divide: \( 10.41 \div 3 = 3.47 \)

Problem 2
Four tablespoons equals \(\frac{1}{4}\) cup. How many tablespoons would equal one full cup?
Answer: 16 tablespoons

We know that 4 tablespoons = \(\frac{1}{4}\) cup.

To get 1 full cup, we need four quarters (\(\frac{4}{4}\)).

Multiply the amount of tablespoons by 4: \( 4 \times 4 = 16 \).

Problem 3
The temperature on the moon ranges from a high of about \(130^\circ\text{C}\) to a low of about \(-110^\circ\text{C}\). This is a difference of how many degrees?
Answer: 240 degrees

To find the difference, subtract the lower temperature from the higher temperature.

\( 130 - (-110) \)

Subtracting a negative is the same as adding: \( 130 + 110 = 240 \).

Problem 4
Four of the 12 marbles in the bag are blue. If one marble is taken from the bag, what is the probability that the marble is:
a. blue?
b. not blue?
c. What word names the relationship between the events in a and b?
Answers: a. \(\frac{1}{3}\), b. \(\frac{2}{3}\), c. Complementary

a. Probability(Blue) = \(\frac{\text{blue marbles}}{\text{total marbles}} = \frac{4}{12}\). Reducing the fraction gives \(\frac{1}{3}\).

b. Probability(Not Blue) = \(\frac{\text{non-blue marbles}}{\text{total marbles}}\). If 4 are blue, \(12 - 4 = 8\) are not blue. \(\frac{8}{12}\) reduces to \(\frac{2}{3}\).

c. The events are complementary because their probabilities add up to 1 (\( \frac{1}{3} + \frac{2}{3} = 1 \)) and they cover all possible outcomes.

Problem 5
The diameter of a circle is 1 meter. The circumference is how many centimeters? (Use 3.14 for \(\pi\).)
Answer: 314 cm

1. Convert diameter to centimeters: \(1 \text{ meter} = 100 \text{ cm}\).

2. Formula for circumference: \( C = \pi \times d \).

3. Calculate: \( C = 3.14 \times 100 = 314 \text{ cm}\).

Problem 6
What fraction of a dollar is a nickel?
Answer: \(\frac{1}{20}\)

A nickel is 5 cents. A dollar is 100 cents.

Fraction: \(\frac{5}{100}\).

Divide numerator and denominator by 5 to simplify: \(\frac{1}{20}\).

Problem 7
Find the unknown number: \( n - \frac{1}{2} = \frac{3}{6} \)
Answer: \(n = 1\)

First, simplify \(\frac{3}{6}\) to \(\frac{1}{2}\).

The equation becomes: \( n - \frac{1}{2} = \frac{1}{2} \).

Add \(\frac{1}{2}\) to both sides: \( n = \frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{2} = 1 \).

Problem 8
Find the unknown number: \( 1 - w = \frac{7}{12} \)
Answer: \(w = \frac{5}{12}\)

Rewrite 1 as a fraction with a denominator of 12: \( \frac{12}{12} \).

Equation: \( \frac{12}{12} - w = \frac{7}{12} \).

So, \( w = \frac{12}{12} - \frac{7}{12} = \frac{5}{12} \).

Problem 9
Find the unknown number: \( w + 2\frac{1}{2} = 3\frac{1}{3} \)
Answer: \(w = \frac{5}{6}\)

Subtract \(2\frac{1}{2}\) from both sides: \( w = 3\frac{1}{3} - 2\frac{1}{2} \).

Find a common denominator (6).
\( 3\frac{1}{3} = 3\frac{2}{6} \)
\( 2\frac{1}{2} = 2\frac{3}{6} \)

We need to subtract \(2\frac{3}{6}\) from \(3\frac{2}{6}\). Since \(\frac{3}{6}\) is larger than \(\frac{2}{6}\), borrow 1 from the 3.

\(3\frac{2}{6}\) becomes \(2\frac{8}{6}\).

\( 2\frac{8}{6} - 2\frac{3}{6} = \frac{5}{6} \).

Problem 11
Write the standard decimal number for: \( (6 \times 10) + (4 \times \frac{1}{10}) + (3 \times \frac{1}{100}) \)
Answer: 60.43

\(6 \times 10 = 60\)

\(4 \times \frac{1}{10} = 0.4\)

\(3 \times \frac{1}{100} = 0.03\)

Add them together: \(60 + 0.4 + 0.03 = 60.43\).

Problem 12
Which of these numbers is closest to 1?
A. -1     B. 0.1     C. 10
Answer: B (0.1)

Find the distance of each number from 1:

  • A (-1): Distance is \( |1 - (-1)| = 2 \)
  • B (0.1): Distance is \( |1 - 0.1| = 0.9 \)
  • C (10): Distance is \( |1 - 10| = 9 \)

0.9 is the smallest distance, so 0.1 is closest.

Problem 13
What is the largest prime number that is less than 100?
Answer: 97

Check odd numbers counting down from 99.

99 is divisible by 3 and 9.

97 is not divisible by 2, 3, 5, or 7. It is prime.

Problem 14
Which of these figures is not a parallelogram?
A. (Rectangle)   B. (Parallelogram)   C. (Trapezoid)   D. (Square)
Answer: C

A parallelogram must have two pairs of parallel sides.

Figure C is a trapezoid, which has only one pair of parallel sides.

Problem 15
A loop of string two feet around is formed to make a square.
a. How many inches long is each side of the square?
b. What is the area of the square in square inches?
Answer: a. 6 inches, b. 36 sq inches

a. The perimeter is 2 feet. Convert to inches: \( 2 \times 12 = 24 \) inches.
A square has 4 equal sides. \( 24 \div 4 = 6 \) inches.

b. Area = side \(\times\) side. \( 6 \times 6 = 36 \) square inches.

Problem 16
Figure ABCD is a rectangle. (Refer to figure in book where M is on line AB).
a. Name an angle complementary to \(\angle DCM\).
b. Name an angle supplementary to \(\angle AMC\).
Answers: a. \(\angle BCM\), b. \(\angle BMC\)

a. Angle C of the rectangle is \(90^\circ\). Since M is a point such that a line is drawn to C, \(\angle DCM\) and \(\angle BCM\) (or \(\angle MCB\)) add up to make the corner angle. Thus, they are complementary.

b. M is on the straight line AB. Angles on a straight line add to \(180^\circ\). Therefore, \(\angle AMC\) and \(\angle BMC\) are supplementary.

Problem 21
Write the prime factorization of both the numerator and the denominator of this fraction. Then reduce the fraction: \( \frac{72}{120} \)
Answer: \(\frac{3}{5}\)

Prime Factorization of 72: \( 8 \times 9 = 2^3 \times 3^2 = 2 \cdot 2 \cdot 2 \cdot 3 \cdot 3 \)

Prime Factorization of 120: \( 10 \times 12 = (2 \cdot 5) \times (4 \cdot 3) = 2 \cdot 2 \cdot 2 \cdot 3 \cdot 5 \)

Reduce:

\( \frac{2 \cdot 2 \cdot 2 \cdot 3 \cdot 3}{2 \cdot 2 \cdot 2 \cdot 3 \cdot 5} \)

Cancel three 2s and one 3. You are left with \( \frac{3}{5} \).

Problem 24
Draw a pair of parallel lines. Then draw a second pair of parallel lines perpendicular to the first pair... Trace the quadrilateral formed. Is the quadrilateral a rectangle?
Answer: Yes

If you draw two parallel lines, and then two lines perpendicular to them, all intersection angles will be \(90^\circ\).

A quadrilateral with four right angles is, by definition, a rectangle.

Problem 25
Simplify: \( \frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{5}{6} \cdot \frac{3}{5} \)
Answer: \(\frac{1}{4}\)

Multiply numerators: \( 1 \times 5 \times 3 = 15 \)

Multiply denominators: \( 2 \times 6 \times 5 = 60 \)

Fraction: \( \frac{15}{60} \). Divide both by 15 to reduce to \( \frac{1}{4} \).

Tip: You can cancel the 5s and the 3 with the 6 before multiplying.

Problem 26
Simplify: \( 3 \times 1\frac{1}{2} \times 2\frac{2}{3} \)
Answer: 12

Convert to improper fractions:

\( 3 = \frac{3}{1} \)

\( 1\frac{1}{2} = \frac{3}{2} \)

\( 2\frac{2}{3} = \frac{8}{3} \)

Multiply: \( \frac{3}{1} \times \frac{3}{2} \times \frac{8}{3} \)

Cancel the 3 in numerator and denominator. \( \frac{3 \times 8}{2} = \frac{24}{2} = 12 \).

Real-World Connection
LaDonna had errands... She calculated she would spend 20 minutes in the post office, 10 minutes at the hardware store, 5 minutes at the cleaners, and 30 minutes eating lunch. The meter read: $0.25 = 15 min, $0.10 = 6 min, $0.05 = 3 min.

a. How much time will LaDonna spend to finish doing her errands?
b. If the meter has ten minutes left, how much money will she need to put into the meter?
Answer: a. 65 minutes (1 hr 5 min), b. $0.95 (approx)

a. Total Time: \( 20 + 10 + 5 + 30 = 65 \) minutes.

b. Money Needed:

She needs 65 minutes total. The meter has 10 minutes already. She needs to buy \( 65 - 10 = 55 \) minutes.

Let's calculate the cost using the rate of $0.05 per 3 minutes (which is the base rate for all options).

55 minutes is not perfectly divisible by 3, so she needs to pay for enough time to cover 55 mins. \( 55 \div 3 = 18.33 \). She needs 19 "units" of 3 minutes (57 mins).

\( 19 \times 0.05 = \$0.95 \).

Alternatively, she could use combinations: 3 quarters (45 min) + 1 dime (6 min) + 2 nickels (6 min) = 57 minutes for $0.95.
