30 Question Math Review

Topics: Reducing Fractions, Midpoints, Decimal Division, Equations, Geometry, Mixed Numbers.

1. Reduce Before Multiplying (Topics 4 & 6)

1. Multiply: \( \frac{3}{8} \cdot \frac{4}{9} \)
Reduce 3 and 9 (divide by 3) → 1 and 3.
Reduce 4 and 8 (divide by 4) → 1 and 2.
\( \frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{1}{3} \)
Answer: \( \frac{1}{6} \)
2. Multiply: \( \frac{5}{12} \cdot \frac{6}{15} \)
Reduce 6 and 12 (divide by 6) → 1 and 2.
Reduce 5 and 15 (divide by 5) → 1 and 3.
\( \frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{1}{3} \)
Answer: \( \frac{1}{6} \)
3. Multiply: \( \frac{9}{10} \cdot \frac{2}{3} \)
Reduce 9 and 3 → 3 and 1.
Reduce 2 and 10 → 1 and 5.
\( \frac{3}{5} \cdot \frac{1}{1} \)
Answer: \( \frac{3}{5} \)
4. Multiply: \( \frac{7}{8} \cdot \frac{16}{21} \)
Reduce 7 and 21 → 1 and 3.
Reduce 16 and 8 → 2 and 1.
\( \frac{1}{1} \cdot \frac{2}{3} \)
Answer: \( \frac{2}{3} \)
5. Multiply: \( \frac{4}{5} \cdot \frac{10}{12} \)
Reduce 10 and 5 → 2 and 1.
Reduce 4 and 12 → 1 and 3.
\( \frac{1}{1} \cdot \frac{2}{3} \)
Answer: \( \frac{2}{3} \)

2. Midpoints & Averages (Topic 7)

6. Find the number halfway between 0.4 and 0.6.
Add: \( 0.4 + 0.6 = 1.0 \).
Divide by 2: \( 1.0 \div 2 \).
Answer: 0.5
7. Find the number halfway between \( \frac{1}{5} \) and \( \frac{3}{5} \).
Add: \( \frac{1}{5} + \frac{3}{5} = \frac{4}{5} \).
Divide by 2: \( \frac{4}{5} \cdot \frac{1}{2} = \frac{4}{10} \).
Answer: \( \frac{2}{5} \)
8. Find the midpoint of 10 and 20.
Add: \( 10 + 20 = 30 \).
Divide by 2: \( 30 \div 2 \).
Answer: 15
9. Find the midpoint of 1.5 and 2.5.
Add: \( 1.5 + 2.5 = 4.0 \).
Divide by 2: \( 4 \div 2 \).
Answer: 2

4. Solving Fraction Equations (Topic 19)

15. Solve for y: \( y - \frac{2}{3} = \frac{1}{6} \)
Add \( \frac{2}{3} \) to both sides. (\( \frac{2}{3} = \frac{4}{6} \)).
\( \frac{1}{6} + \frac{4}{6} = \frac{5}{6} \).
Answer: \( y = \frac{5}{6} \)
16. Solve for x: \( x - \frac{1}{4} = \frac{3}{4} \)
Add \( \frac{1}{4} \) to both sides.
\( \frac{3}{4} + \frac{1}{4} = \frac{4}{4} \).
Answer: \( x = 1 \)
17. Solve for n: \( n - \frac{1}{2} = \frac{5}{8} \)
Convert \( \frac{1}{2} \) to \( \frac{4}{8} \). Add to \( \frac{5}{8} \).
\( \frac{5}{8} + \frac{4}{8} = \frac{9}{8} \).
Answer: \( n = 1\frac{1}{8} \)
18. Solve for m: \( m - \frac{3}{5} = \frac{1}{10} \)
Convert \( \frac{3}{5} \) to \( \frac{6}{10} \). Add to \( \frac{1}{10} \).
\( \frac{1}{10} + \frac{6}{10} = \frac{7}{10} \).
Answer: \( m = \frac{7}{10} \)
19. Solve for a: \( a - \frac{7}{8} = \frac{1}{8} \)
Add \( \frac{7}{8} \) to both sides.
\( \frac{1}{8} + \frac{7}{8} = \frac{8}{8} \).
Answer: \( a = 1 \)

5. Geometry Solids (Topic 20)

20. How many vertices does a Cube have?
Count the corners (vertices): 4 on top, 4 on bottom.
Answer: 8 vertices
21. How many faces does a Rectangular Prism have?
It's a box shape (like a cereal box). Top, bottom, front, back, left, right.
Answer: 6 faces
22. How many edges does a Square Pyramid have?
Base is a square (4 edges). 4 edges go up to the peak.
Answer: 8 edges
23. What is the shape of the base of a Triangular Prism?
A prism is named after its base shape.
Answer: A Triangle
24. How many faces does a Triangular Pyramid have?
It has one triangle base and 3 triangular sides meeting at the top.
Answer: 4 faces
25. How many edges does a Cube have?
4 on the top square, 4 on the bottom square, 4 vertical connecting edges.
Answer: 12 edges

6. Multiplying Mixed Numbers (Topic 21)

26. Multiply: \( 1\frac{1}{3} \times 1\frac{1}{2} \)
Convert: \( \frac{4}{3} \times \frac{3}{2} \).
Cancel 3s. Reduce 4 and 2.
Answer: 2
27. Multiply: \( 2\frac{1}{4} \times 1\frac{1}{3} \)
Convert: \( \frac{9}{4} \times \frac{4}{3} \).
Cancel 4s. Reduce 9/3.
Answer: 3
28. Multiply: \( 1\frac{1}{5} \times 3\frac{1}{3} \)
Convert: \( \frac{6}{5} \times \frac{10}{3} \).
Reduce 6 and 3 (to 2 and 1). Reduce 10 and 5 (to 2 and 1).
\( 2 \times 2 = 4 \).
Answer: 4
29. Multiply: \( 4\frac{1}{2} \times 1\frac{1}{3} \)
Convert: \( \frac{9}{2} \times \frac{4}{3} \).
Reduce 9 and 3 (to 3 and 1). Reduce 4 and 2 (to 2 and 1).
\( 3 \times 2 = 6 \).
Answer: 6
30. Multiply: \( 1\frac{2}{3} \times 2\frac{1}{5} \)
Convert: \( \frac{5}{3} \times \frac{11}{5} \).
Cancel 5s. Result: \( \frac{11}{3} \).
Answer: \( 3\frac{2}{3} \)
