Lesson Practice

a. Find the value of \(10^4\)

The exponent 4 tells us to multiply the base (10) by itself 4 times.

$$10^4 = 10 \times 10 \times 10 \times 10 = 10,000$$
b. Find the value of \(2^3 + 2^4\)

First, evaluate the exponents:

$$2^3 = 2 \times 2 \times 2 = 8$$ $$2^4 = 2 \times 2 \times 2 \times 2 = 16$$

Then add the results:

$$8 + 16 = 24$$
c. Find the value of \(2^2 \cdot 5^2\)

Evaluate the exponents first:

$$2^2 = 4$$ $$5^2 = 25$$

Then multiply:

$$4 \cdot 25 = 100$$
d. Write the prime factorization of 72 using exponents.

First, find the prime factors of 72:

$$72 = 8 \times 9$$ $$8 = 2 \times 2 \times 2 = 2^3$$ $$9 = 3 \times 3 = 3^2$$

Combine them:

$$72 = 2^3 \cdot 3^2$$
Write each decimal number as a fraction or mixed number:

e. 12.5   f. 1.25   g. 0.125   h. 0.05   i. 0.24   j. 10.2

  • e. 12.5: \(12 \frac{5}{10} = 12 \frac{1}{2}\)
  • f. 1.25: \(1 \frac{25}{100} = 1 \frac{1}{4}\)
  • g. 0.125: \(\frac{125}{1000} = \frac{1}{8}\)
  • h. 0.05: \(\frac{5}{100} = \frac{1}{20}\)
  • i. 0.24: \(\frac{24}{100} = \frac{6}{25}\)
  • j. 10.2: \(10 \frac{2}{10} = 10 \frac{1}{5}\)

Written Practice

1. Tomas's temperature was \(102^\circ F\). Normal body temperature is \(98.6^\circ F\). How many degrees above normal was Tomas's temperature? Write an equation and solve.

Let \(d\) be the difference in temperature.

Equation: \(98.6 + d = 102\) or \(d = 102 - 98.6\)

Solve: \(102.0 - 98.6 = 3.4\)

Answer: \(3.4^\circ F\)

2. Jill has read 42 pages of a 180-page book. How many pages are left for her to read? Write an equation and solve.

Let \(p\) be the pages left.

Equation: \(42 + p = 180\) or \(p = 180 - 42\)

Solve: \(180 - 42 = 138\)

Answer: 138 pages

3. If Jill wants to finish the book in the next three days, then she should read an average of how many pages per day?

Using the result from Problem 2 (138 pages left) and dividing by 3 days:

Equation: \(138 \div 3 = x\)

$$ \frac{138}{3} = 46 $$

Answer: 46 pages per day

4. Write 2.5 as a reduced mixed number.
$$ 2.5 = 2 \frac{5}{10} $$

Reduce the fraction \(\frac{5}{10}\) to \(\frac{1}{2}\).

Answer: \(2 \frac{1}{2}\)

5. Write 0.35 as a reduced fraction.
$$ 0.35 = \frac{35}{100} $$

Divide numerator and denominator by 5:

$$ \frac{35 \div 5}{100 \div 5} = \frac{7}{20} $$

Answer: \(\frac{7}{20}\)

6. What is the total cost of a $12.60 item when \(7\frac{1}{2}\%\) (0.075) sales tax is added?

First, calculate the tax amount:

$$ \$12.60 \times 0.075 = \$0.945 $$

Round tax to the nearest cent: \(\$0.95\)

Add tax to the original price:

$$ \$12.60 + \$0.95 = \$13.55 $$

Answer: $13.55

7. \(\frac{3}{4} \times 2 \times 1\frac{1}{3}\)

Convert whole numbers and mixed numbers to improper fractions:

$$ \frac{3}{4} \times \frac{2}{1} \times \frac{4}{3} $$

Multiply straight across or cancel out. Notice the 3 in the numerator and denominator cancel out, and the 4s cancel out.

$$ \frac{3}{4} \times \frac{4}{3} \times \frac{2}{1} = 1 \times 2 = 2 $$

Answer: 2

8. \((100 - 10^2) \div 5^2\)

Order of operations (PEMDAS):

1. Exponents inside parenthesis: \(10^2 = 100\)

$$ (100 - 100) \div 5^2 $$

2. Parenthesis subtraction: \(100 - 100 = 0\)

$$ 0 \div 5^2 $$

3. Exponent: \(5^2 = 25\)

$$ 0 \div 25 = 0 $$

Answer: 0

9. \(3 + 2\frac{1}{3} + 1\frac{3}{4}\)

Find a common denominator for 3 and 4, which is 12.

$$ 2\frac{1}{3} = 2\frac{4}{12} $$ $$ 1\frac{3}{4} = 1\frac{9}{12} $$

Add whole numbers: \(3 + 2 + 1 = 6\)

Add fractions: \(\frac{4}{12} + \frac{9}{12} = \frac{13}{12} = 1\frac{1}{12}\)

Combine: \(6 + 1\frac{1}{12} = 7\frac{1}{12}\)

Answer: \(7\frac{1}{12}\)

10. \(5\frac{1}{6} - 3\frac{1}{2}\)

Find a common denominator (6).

$$ 3\frac{1}{2} = 3\frac{3}{6} $$

Set up subtraction: \(5\frac{1}{6} - 3\frac{3}{6}\)

We cannot subtract \(\frac{3}{6}\) from \(\frac{1}{6}\), so we borrow from the 5.

$$ 5\frac{1}{6} = 4\frac{7}{6} $$ $$ 4\frac{7}{6} - 3\frac{3}{6} = 1\frac{4}{6} $$

Reduce fraction: \(1\frac{4}{6} = 1\frac{2}{3}\)

Answer: \(1\frac{2}{3}\)

11. \(\frac{3}{4} \div 1\frac{1}{2}\)

Convert mixed number to improper fraction:

$$ 1\frac{1}{2} = \frac{3}{2} $$

To divide by a fraction, multiply by its reciprocal:

$$ \frac{3}{4} \times \frac{2}{3} $$

Cancel out 3s and simplify \(\frac{2}{4}\):

$$ \frac{1}{2} $$

Answer: \(\frac{1}{2}\)

12. \(7 \div 0.4\)

Move the decimal one place to the right for both numbers to divide by a whole number:

$$ 70 \div 4 $$ $$ 70 \div 4 = 17.5 $$

Answer: 17.5

13. Compare:
a. \(5^2 \bigcirc 2^5\)
b. \(0.3 \bigcirc 0.125\)

a. \(5^2 = 25\) and \(2^5 = 32\). Since \(25 < 32\), the answer is <.

b. Write 0.3 as 0.300 to compare easily with 0.125. Since 300 > 125, the answer is >.

14. The diameter of a quarter is about 2.4 cm.
a. What is the circumference? (\(\pi = 3.14\))
b. What is the ratio of the radius to the diameter?

a. Circumference formula: \(C = \pi d\)

$$ C = 3.14 \times 2.4 = 7.536 \text{ cm} $$

b. The radius is always half of the diameter.

Ratio radius : diameter = \(r : 2r\) = 1 : 2

15. Find the unknown: \(25m = 0.175\)

Divide both sides by 25:

$$ m = \frac{0.175}{25} $$

Calculation: \(0.175 \div 25 = 0.007\)

Answer: \(m = 0.007\)

16. Find the unknown: \(1.2 + y + 4.25 = 7\)

First combine like terms:

$$ 1.2 + 4.25 = 5.45 $$ $$ 5.45 + y = 7 $$

Subtract 5.45 from 7:

$$ y = 7 - 5.45 = 1.55 $$

Answer: \(y = 1.55\)

17. Which digit is in the ten-thousands place in 123,456.78?

Place values starting from decimal point leftwards: Ones (6), Tens (5), Hundreds (4), Thousands (3), Ten-Thousands (2).

Answer: 2

18. Arrange these numbers from least to greatest: \(1, \frac{1}{2}, \frac{1}{10}, \frac{1}{4}, 0\)

Convert to decimals for easier comparison:

  • 1 = 1.0
  • \(\frac{1}{2} = 0.5\)
  • \(\frac{1}{10} = 0.1\)
  • \(\frac{1}{4} = 0.25\)
  • 0 = 0

Order: 0, 0.1, 0.25, 0.5, 1

Answer: \(0, \frac{1}{10}, \frac{1}{4}, \frac{1}{2}, 1\)

19. Write the prime factorization of 200 using exponents.

Factor tree for 200:

$$ 200 = 2 \times 100 $$ $$ 100 = 10 \times 10 = (2 \times 5) \times (2 \times 5) $$

Gather all primes: \(2, 2, 5, 2, 5\)

Group them: \(2^3 \times 5^2\)

Answer: \(2^3 \cdot 5^2\)

20. The store offered a 20% discount. Regular price was $18.00.
a. How much is the discount?
b. What is the new price?

a. \(20\%\) of \(18.00 = 0.2 \times 18 = 3.6\). Discount is $3.60.

b. New Price = \(18.00 - 3.60 = 14.4\). New price is $14.40.

21. The length of AB is 16 mm. The length of AC is 50 mm. What is the length of BC?

Points A, B, and C are on a line.

\(AC = AB + BC\)

\(50 = 16 + BC\)

\(BC = 50 - 16 = 34\)

Answer: 34 mm

22. One half of the area of this square is shaded. What is the area of the shaded region? (Side length = 6 in)

Area of the full square = \(side \times side = 6 \times 6 = 36 \text{ sq in}\).

Shaded area is half of the total area.

$$ 36 \div 2 = 18 $$

Answer: 18 sq in.

23. Verify: Is every square a rectangle?

Answer: Yes.

A rectangle is defined as a quadrilateral with four right angles. A square has four right angles and equal sides. Therefore, a square fits the definition of a rectangle (it is a special type of rectangle).

24. Analyze: \(\frac{2^2 + 2^3}{2}\)

Calculate numerator first:

$$ 2^2 = 4 $$ $$ 2^3 = 8 $$ $$ 4 + 8 = 12 $$

Now divide:

$$ \frac{12}{2} = 6 $$

Answer: 6

25. Explain "fraction form" for multiplying fractions.

When multiplying fractions, especially with mixed numbers, we convert all numbers into improper fractions. This "fraction form" (numerator over denominator) allows us to multiply numerators together and denominators together easily.

30. The measure of \(\angle W\) in parallelogram WXYZ is \(75^\circ\).
a. Measure of \(\angle X\)?
b. Measure of \(\angle Y\)?

In a parallelogram:

Consecutive angles (next to each other) add up to \(180^\circ\).

Opposite angles are equal.

a. \(\angle X\): \(180^\circ - 75^\circ = 105^\circ\)

b. \(\angle Y\): Opposite to \(\angle W\), so it is equal. \(75^\circ\)
