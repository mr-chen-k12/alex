Lesson Practice

a. Reduce before multiplying: \( \frac{3}{4} \cdot \frac{4}{5} \)
We can cancel the 4 in the numerator and the denominator.
$$ \frac{3}{\bcancel{4}} \cdot \frac{\bcancel{4}}{5} = \frac{3}{1} \cdot \frac{1}{5} $$
Answer: \( \frac{3}{5} \)
b. Reduce before multiplying: \( \frac{2}{3} \cdot \frac{3}{4} \)
Cancel the 3s (one on top, one on bottom). Reduce 2 and 4 by dividing both by 2.
$$ \frac{\bcancel{2}^1}{\bcancel{3}_1} \cdot \frac{\bcancel{3}^1}{\bcancel{4}_2} = \frac{1}{1} \cdot \frac{1}{2} $$
Answer: \( \frac{1}{2} \)
c. Reduce before multiplying: \( \frac{8}{9} \cdot \frac{9}{10} \)
Cancel the 9s. Reduce 8 and 10 by dividing both by 2.
$$ \frac{\bcancel{8}^4}{\bcancel{9}_1} \cdot \frac{\bcancel{9}^1}{\bcancel{10}_5} = \frac{4}{1} \cdot \frac{1}{5} $$
Answer: \( \frac{4}{5} \)
d. Write in fraction form, then reduce: \( 2\frac{1}{4} \times 4 \)
Convert mixed number to improper fraction: \( 2\frac{1}{4} = \frac{9}{4} \). Write 4 as \( \frac{4}{1} \).
$$ \frac{9}{4} \cdot \frac{4}{1} $$
Cancel the 4s.
Answer: 9
e. Write in fraction form, then reduce: \( 1\frac{1}{2} \times 2\frac{2}{3} \)
Convert to improper fractions: \( 1\frac{1}{2} = \frac{3}{2} \) and \( 2\frac{2}{3} = \frac{8}{3} \).
$$ \frac{3}{2} \cdot \frac{8}{3} $$
Cancel the 3s. Reduce 8 and 2 (\(8 \div 2 = 4\)).
Answer: 4
f. Write in fraction form, then reduce: \( 3\frac{1}{3} \times 2\frac{1}{4} \)
Convert: \( 3\frac{1}{3} = \frac{10}{3} \) and \( 2\frac{1}{4} = \frac{9}{4} \).
$$ \frac{10}{3} \cdot \frac{9}{4} $$
Reduce 10 and 4 (by 2) -> 5 and 2. Reduce 9 and 3 (by 3) -> 3 and 1.
$$ \frac{5}{1} \cdot \frac{3}{2} = \frac{15}{2} $$
Answer: \( 7\frac{1}{2} \)
g. Rewrite division as multiplication: \( \frac{2}{5} \div \frac{2}{3} \)
Multiply by the reciprocal of the second fraction: \( \frac{2}{5} \cdot \frac{3}{2} \).
Cancel the 2s.
Answer: \( \frac{3}{5} \)
h. Rewrite division as multiplication: \( \frac{8}{9} \div \frac{2}{3} \)
Multiply by reciprocal: \( \frac{8}{9} \cdot \frac{3}{2} \).
Reduce 8 and 2 -> 4 and 1. Reduce 3 and 9 -> 1 and 3.
\( \frac{4}{3} \cdot \frac{1}{1} = \frac{4}{3} \)
Answer: \( 1\frac{1}{3} \)
i. Rewrite division as multiplication: \( \frac{9}{10} \div 1\frac{1}{5} \)
Convert mixed number: \( 1\frac{1}{5} = \frac{6}{5} \).
Rewrite: \( \frac{9}{10} \div \frac{6}{5} = \frac{9}{10} \cdot \frac{5}{6} \).
Reduce 9 and 6 (by 3) -> 3 and 2. Reduce 5 and 10 (by 5) -> 1 and 2.
\( \frac{3}{2} \cdot \frac{1}{2} = \frac{3}{4} \)
Answer: \( \frac{3}{4} \)

2. How many eighth notes equal a half note?
A half note is \( \frac{1}{2} \). An eighth note is \( \frac{1}{8} \).
\( \frac{1}{2} \div \frac{1}{8} = \frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{8}{1} = 4 \)

Answer: 5

26. Prime factorization of 72/300, then reduce.
Numerator 72: \( 8 \times 9 = 2^3 \times 3^2 \).
Denominator 300: \( 3 \times 100 = 3 \times 10^2 = 3 \times (2 \times 5)^2 = 2^2 \times 3 \times 5^2 \).
\( \frac{2 \cdot 2 \cdot 2 \cdot 3 \cdot 3}{2 \cdot 2 \cdot 3 \cdot 5 \cdot 5} \)
Cancel \( 2^2 \) and one 3. Remaining: Top \( 2 \cdot 3 \), Bottom \( 5 \cdot 5 \).
Answer: \( \frac{6}{25} \)

Real-World Connection

Roland's Shopping Trip
Roland bought a roller ($8.97), milk ($2.89), magazine ($1.59), and two identical gallons of paint. Total was $47.83. Find the price of each gallon.
1. Sum of known items: \( 8.97 + 2.89 + 1.59 = 13.45 \).
2. Subtract from total to find cost of paint: \( 47.83 - 13.45 = 34.38 \).
3. Divide by 2 gallons: \( 34.38 \div 2 = 17.19 \).
Answer: $17.19 per gallon
