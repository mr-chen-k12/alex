1. Solve for \( x \) in the quadratic equation \( 2x^2 - 4x - 6 = 0 \).

Solution:

First, we can divide the entire equation by 2 to simplify: $$ x^2 - 2x - 3 = 0 $$
We factor the quadratic: $$ (x - 3)(x + 1) = 0 $$
Therefore, the possible values for \( x \) are: $$ x = 3 \quad \text{or} \quad x = -1 $$
