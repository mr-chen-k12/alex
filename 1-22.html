Problem 1: Projectile Motion

A ball is launched from the ground with an initial velocity of $v_0 = 50 \, \text{m/s}$ at an angle of $\theta = 30^\circ$ above the horizontal. Calculate the total time of flight ($t$) and the maximum height ($H$) reached. Assume $g = 9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2$ and ignore air resistance.

Explanation:

Step 1: Break down the velocity vector.
The initial velocity components are: $$v_{0x} = v_0 \cos(\theta) = 50 \cos(30^\circ) \approx 43.3 \, \text{m/s}$$ $$v_{0y} = v_0 \sin(\theta) = 50 \sin(30^\circ) = 25 \, \text{m/s}$$
Step 2: Calculate Time of Flight ($t$).
The total time is determined by the vertical motion. The ball lands when vertical displacement is zero. $$y = v_{0y}t - \frac{1}{2}gt^2 = 0 \implies t(v_{0y} - \frac{1}{2}gt) = 0$$ $$t = \frac{2v_{0y}}{g} = \frac{2(25)}{9.8} \approx 5.10 \, \text{s}$$
Step 3: Calculate Maximum Height ($H$).
Max height occurs when $v_y = 0$. $$v_y^2 = v_{0y}^2 - 2gH \implies 0 = (25)^2 - 2(9.8)H$$ $$H = \frac{625}{19.6} \approx 31.89 \, \text{m}$$
Final Answer: Time of flight is 5.10 s and Maximum Height is 31.89 m.
