1. Word Problem: Fractions & Cost
Marcus is building a birdhouse. He needs \(3\) feet of oak, \(\frac{1}{2}\) foot of pine, and \(\frac{1}{4}\) foot of cedar. The wood costs $4.00 per foot. How much will the wood cost in total?
Step 1: Find the total length of wood needed.
$$ 3 + \frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{4} = 3 + 0.5 + 0.25 = 3.75 \text{ feet} $$
Step 2: Multiply the total length by the cost per foot.
$$ 3.75 \times \$4.00 = \$15.00 $$
Answer: $15.00
2. Estimation
A standard ton is 2,000 pounds. A metric ton is approximately 2,205 pounds. About how many more pounds is a metric ton than a standard ton?
Subtract the standard ton from the metric ton.
$$ 2205 - 2000 = 205 $$
Answer: About 205 pounds
3. Equivalent Division
Instead of dividing \(2.40 \div 0.04\), Jenny multiplied both numbers by 100 to form an equivalent division problem with whole numbers. Write the new problem and find the quotient.
Multiply dividend and divisor by 100 to remove decimals.
$$ 2.40 \times 100 = 240 $$ $$ 0.04 \times 100 = 4 $$
Perform division:
$$ 240 \div 4 = 60 $$
Answer: \(240 \div 4 = 60\)

6. Find Unknown (Fraction)
Find \(m\): \( \frac{5}{6} - m = \frac{1}{3} \)
Rearrange to find \(m\): \(m = \frac{5}{6} - \frac{1}{3}\).
Convert \(\frac{1}{3}\) to \(\frac{2}{6}\).
$$ m = \frac{5}{6} - \frac{2}{6} = \frac{3}{6} $$
Answer: \(m = \frac{1}{2}\)

Answer: \( \frac{27}{125} \)
