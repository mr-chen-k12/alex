Lesson Practice

f. Calculate: \( 2 \frac{2}{3} \div 2 \frac{1}{2} \)
Solution: \( 1 \frac{1}{15} \)

Steps:
Convert mixed numbers to improper fractions:
\( 2 \frac{2}{3} = \frac{8}{3} \) and \( 2 \frac{1}{2} = \frac{5}{2} \)
Divide by multiplying by the reciprocal:
\( \frac{8}{3} \div \frac{5}{2} = \frac{8}{3} \times \frac{2}{5} = \frac{16}{15} \)
Convert back to mixed number: \( 1 \frac{1}{15} \)
g. Calculate: \( 2 \frac{1}{2} \div 1 \frac{2}{3} \)
Solution: \( 1 \frac{1}{2} \)

Steps:
\( \frac{5}{2} \div \frac{5}{3} = \frac{5}{2} \times \frac{3}{5} \)
The 5s cancel out: \( \frac{3}{2} = 1 \frac{1}{2} \)
h. Calculate: \( 1 \frac{1}{2} \div 1 \frac{1}{2} \)
Solution: \( 1 \)

Reasoning: Any number divided by itself is 1.
i. Calculate: \( 7 \div 1 \frac{3}{4} \)
Solution: \( 4 \)

Steps:
Convert \( 1 \frac{3}{4} \) to \( \frac{7}{4} \)
\( 7 \div \frac{7}{4} = \frac{7}{1} \times \frac{4}{7} \)
The 7s cancel out, leaving \( 4 \).
j. Gabriel has \( 2 \frac{1}{3} \) hours to finish three projects. If he divides his time equally, what fraction of an hour can he spend on each project?
Solution: \( \frac{7}{9} \) of an hour

Steps:
Total time: \( 2 \frac{1}{3} = \frac{7}{3} \) hours.
Divide by 3: \( \frac{7}{3} \div 3 = \frac{7}{3} \times \frac{1}{3} = \frac{7}{9} \)

Written Practice

1. What is the difference between the sum of \( \frac{1}{2} \) and \( \frac{1}{4} \) and the product of \( \frac{1}{2} \) and \( \frac{1}{4} \)?
Solution: \( \frac{5}{8} \)

Steps:
Sum: \( \frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{4} = \frac{2}{4} + \frac{1}{4} = \frac{3}{4} \)
Product: \( \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{1}{4} = \frac{1}{8} \)
Difference: \( \frac{3}{4} - \frac{1}{8} = \frac{6}{8} - \frac{1}{8} = \frac{5}{8} \)
2. Bill ran a half mile in two minutes fifty-five seconds. How many seconds is that?
Solution: 175 seconds

Steps:
2 minutes = \( 2 \times 60 = 120 \) seconds.
\( 120 + 55 = 175 \) seconds.
3. The gauge of a railroad is 4 feet \( 8\frac{1}{2} \) inches. How many inches is that?
Solution: \( 56\frac{1}{2} \) inches (or 56.5 inches)

Steps:
1 foot = 12 inches.
4 feet = \( 4 \times 12 = 48 \) inches.
Total = \( 48 + 8\frac{1}{2} = 56\frac{1}{2} \) inches.
4. Calculate: \( 1 \frac{1}{2} \div 2 \frac{2}{3} \)
Solution: \( \frac{9}{16} \)

Steps:
\( \frac{3}{2} \div \frac{8}{3} = \frac{3}{2} \times \frac{3}{8} = \frac{9}{16} \)
5. Calculate: \( 1 \frac{1}{3} \div 4 \)
Solution: \( \frac{1}{3} \)

Steps:
\( \frac{4}{3} \div \frac{4}{1} = \frac{4}{3} \times \frac{1}{4} \)
Cancel 4s: \( \frac{1}{3} \)
6. In six games Yvonne scored a total of 108 points. How many points per game did she average?
Solution: 18 points per game

Steps:
\( 108 \div 6 = 18 \)
7. Write the prime factorizations of 24 and 200. Then reduce \( \frac{24}{200} \).
Solution:
24: \( 2 \cdot 2 \cdot 2 \cdot 3 \) (or \( 2^3 \cdot 3 \))
200: \( 2 \cdot 2 \cdot 2 \cdot 5 \cdot 5 \) (or \( 2^3 \cdot 5^2 \))
Reduced Fraction: \( \frac{3}{25} \)

Reducing:
Divide top and bottom by \( 2^3 \) (which is 8).
\( 24 \div 8 = 3 \)
\( 200 \div 8 = 25 \)
8. Find m: \( m - 5\frac{3}{8} = 1\frac{3}{16} \)
Solution: \( m = 6\frac{9}{16} \)

Steps:
\( m = 1\frac{3}{16} + 5\frac{3}{8} \)
Convert \( \frac{3}{8} \) to \( \frac{6}{16} \).
\( 1\frac{3}{16} + 5\frac{6}{16} = 6\frac{9}{16} \)
9. Find n: \( 3\frac{3}{5} + 2\frac{7}{10} = n \)
Solution: \( n = 6\frac{3}{10} \)

Steps:
\( 3\frac{6}{10} + 2\frac{7}{10} = 5\frac{13}{10} \)
\( \frac{13}{10} \) is \( 1\frac{3}{10} \).
\( 5 + 1\frac{3}{10} = 6\frac{3}{10} \)
10. Solve for d: \( 25d = 0.375 \)
Solution: \( d = 0.015 \)

Steps:
\( d = 0.375 \div 25 \)
Using fractions: \( \frac{3}{8} \div 25 = \frac{3}{8} \times \frac{1}{25} = \frac{3}{200} \)
\( \frac{3}{200} = \frac{1.5}{100} = 0.015 \)
11. Solve for w: \( \frac{3}{4} = \frac{w}{100} \)
Solution: \( w = 75 \)

Steps:
\( \frac{3}{4} \) of 100 is 75.
Alternatively, cross multiply: \( 4w = 300 \rightarrow w = 75 \).
12. Calculate: \( 5\frac{1}{8} - 1\frac{1}{2} \)
Solution: \( 3\frac{5}{8} \)

Steps:
Common denominator is 8: \( 5\frac{1}{8} - 1\frac{4}{8} \)
Borrow from 5: \( 4\frac{9}{8} - 1\frac{4}{8} = 3\frac{5}{8} \)

Solution: \( 5 \)

Steps:
\( \frac{10}{3} \times \frac{3}{2} \)
The 3s cancel: \( \frac{10}{2} = 5 \)

Steps:
\( \frac{10}{3} \div \frac{3}{2} = \frac{10}{3} \times \frac{2}{3} = \frac{20}{9} = 2\frac{2}{9} \)

Solution: 7 square inches

Steps:
\( \text{Area} = 4 \times 1\frac{3}{4} \)
\( 4 \times \frac{7}{4} = 7 \)

\( 4.2 - 4.2 = 0 \)

18. ANALYZE: The product \( 3.2 \times 10 \) equals which of the following?
A) \( 32 \div 10 \)    B) \( 320 \div 10 \)    C) \( 0.32 \div 10 \)

19. Find the sum of 6416, 5734, and 4912 to the nearest thousand.
Solution: 17,000

Steps:
Exact sum: \( 6416 + 5734 + 4912 = 17,062 \)
Rounding 17,062 to the nearest thousand gives 17,000.
(Alternatively, rounding addends first: \( 6000 + 6000 + 5000 = 17,000 \))
20. Instead of dividing 800 by 24, Arturo formed an equivalent division problem by dividing both the dividend and the divisor by 8. Then he quickly found the quotient. What is the equivalent problem formed, and what is the quotient as a mixed number?
Equivalent Problem: \( 100 \div 3 \)
Quotient: \( 33 \frac{1}{3} \)

Steps:
Dividend: \( 800 \div 8 = 100 \)
Divisor: \( 24 \div 8 = 3 \)
Problem: \( \frac{100}{3} = 33 \text{ R } 1 \rightarrow 33 \frac{1}{3} \)
21. The perimeter of a square is 2.4 meters.
a. How long is each side?
b. What is the area?
a. Side length: 0.6 meters
b. Area: 0.36 square meters

Steps:

b. \( 0.6 \times 0.6 = 0.36 \)
22. What is the tax on an $18,000 car if the tax rate is 8%?
Solution: $1,440

Steps:
\( 18,000 \times 0.08 \)
\( 180 \times 8 = 1,440 \)
23. If the probability of an event occurring is 1 chance in a million, then what is the probability of the event not occurring?
Solution: \( \frac{999,999}{1,000,000} \)

Reasoning: Probability of "not" = 1 - Probability of "occurring".
24. Why is a circle not a polygon?
Solution: Because a polygon must be formed by straight line segments. A circle is curved.

Solution: \( = \) (Equal)

Reasoning: Dividing by 3 is mathematically identical to multiplying by \( \frac{1}{3} \).

27. Which angle in this figure is an obtuse angle?
(Referring to figure with points W, M, Y on line and X above)
Solution: \( \angle WMX \) (Angle WMX)

Reasoning:
\( \angle XMY \) is acute (less than 90°).
\( \angle WMY \) is a straight angle (180°).
\( \angle WMX \) is open wider than a right angle, making it obtuse.

30. Sunrise occurred at 6:20 a.m., and sunset occurred at 5:45 p.m. How many hours and minutes were there from sunrise to sunset?
Solution: 11 hours and 25 minutes

Steps:
From 6:20 a.m. to 5:20 p.m. is 11 hours.
From 5:20 p.m. to 5:45 p.m. is 25 minutes.
Total: 11 hrs 25 mins.
